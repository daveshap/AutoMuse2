After watching her a little, Fitzwilliam asked her       why she was so thoughtful.        “I am thinking of what you have been telling me,” said she. “Your       cousin’s conduct does not suit my feelings. Why was he to be the       judge?”        “You are rather disposed to call his interference officious?”        “I do not see what right Mr. Darcy had to decide on the propriety       of his friend’s inclination, or why, upon his own judgement       alone, he was to determine and direct in what manner his friend       was to be happy.
