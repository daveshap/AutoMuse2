When I see you, my dear Watson, with a week’s accumulation of dust upon your hat, and when your wife allows you to go out in such a state, I shall fear that you also have been unfortunate enough to lose your wife’s affection.”  “But he might be a bachelor.”  “Nay, he was bringing home the goose as a peace-offering to his wife. Remember the card upon the bird’s leg.”  “You have an answer to everything. But how on earth do you deduce that the gas is not laid on in his house?”  “One tallow stain, or even two, might come by chance; but when I see no less than five, I think that there can be little doubt that the individual must be brought into frequent contact with burning tallow—walks upstairs at night probably with his hat in one hand and a guttering candle in the other. Anyhow, he never got tallow-stains from a gas-jet.
